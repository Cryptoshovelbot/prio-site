## Executive Summary

The site is technically healthy at the infrastructure level (sitemap aligned with filesystem, robots.txt allows LLM bots, canonicals consistent, schema rich) but is suffering from a **structural hreflang gap that is almost certainly capping discoverability in Spanish/French markets**. 24 of 38 pages (63%) have no `hreflang` cluster, including all 12 `/es/` city landing pages and 8 EN blog posts. The site is also bleeding momentum: organic impressions dropped from 2,048 (prior 28d) to 1,270 (last 28d) and clicks fell from 12 to 5.

The biggest paradox: pages have outstanding **average positions** (homepage at 4.7, /about/ at 3.7, several blogs at 7-9) but **near-zero CTR** (overall 0.39%). This is a classic SERP-snippet problem — titles/metas don't earn the click despite ranking. Two near-zero-effort wins exist: (1) `barcelona average rent 2026` ranks at position 2.3 with 0 clicks (snippet/title mismatch), and (2) `documents required to rent an apartment in madrid` ranks at 6.1 with 22 impressions but no Madrid-specific section in the EN article.

## 5. Top 3 Priority Actions

### Action 1 — Add hreflang clusters to 12 /es/ city pages + 9 monolingual EN pages (HIGHEST IMPACT)
**Why:** Hreflang absence on 63% of pages means Google can't confidently serve the right page per market. Spain provides 164/1,270 (13%) of impressions but only 2 clicks — hreflang fixes typically lift CTR 15-30% in target markets. /es/ city pages declare lang=en but URL is /es/ — a contradictory signal.

**Files & changes:**
- `/opt/prio-site/es/alicante/index.html` line 2: change `<html lang="en">` to `<html lang="en-ES">` (English content targeted at Spain). Add after line 9 (after canonical):
  ```html
  <link rel="alternate" hreflang="en" href="https://getprio.io/es/alicante/">
  <link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://getprio.io/es/alicante/">
  ```
- Repeat for `/opt/prio-site/es/{barcelona,bilbao,granada,las-palmas,madrid,malaga,palma-de-mallorca,san-sebastian,sevilla,valencia,zaragoza}/index.html`

- `/opt/prio-site/blog/idealista-vs-fotocasa-vs-badi-comparison/index.html` line ~10: add `hreflang="en"` self-ref + `x-default`.
- `/opt/prio-site/blog/barcelona-rental-market-2026/index.html` line ~10: same.
- `/opt/prio-site/blog/idealista-alerts-vs-prio-notification-speed/index.html`, `how-long-find-apartment-barcelona/`, `nie-barcelona-how-to-get/`: same.

## 7. /es/ city pages — strategic decision needed

Two equally valid paths; pick one:

**Option A — Keep English content, rename URL prefix**
- Move `/es/<city>/` to `/cities/<city>/` (301 each)
- Set `<html lang="en">` to match content (already correct)
- Update sitemap, internal links, _redirects

**Option B — Translate to Spanish**
- Translate body, titles, metas to Spanish
- Set `<html lang="es">`
- Add `/cities/` (English) tier targeting expats from outside Spain
- Add hreflang clusters between en+es versions

Option B has higher long-term ceiling (Spain market is the dominant impression source) but is a 12-page translation project. Option A is a 1-day refactor.
