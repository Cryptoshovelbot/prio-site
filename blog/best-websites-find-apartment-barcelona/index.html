Best Websites to Find an Apartment in Barcelona (2026)

June 20269 min read

Barcelona's rental market is one of the most competitive in Europe — a good apartment can collect dozens of enquiries within hours. Knowing which websites to use, and how to combine them, is half the battle. At Prio we monitor the major platforms continuously, so this ranking is based on where listings actually appear, not marketing.

The short answer

Start with Idealista (largest inventory), add Fotocasa (private-landlord exclusives) and Badi (rooms). Together they cover ~95% of listings. The missing piece isn't another website — it's speed: set up real-time alerts so you see new listings before everyone else.

The 10 Best Websites to Find an Apartment in Barcelona

1Idealista — the market leader

Spain's largest property portal and the right starting point for any Barcelona search. It has the most listings, the best filters (metro station, energy rating, elevator, terrace), and a strong app. Best for: everyone — if you use only one site, use this. Watch out for: high listing volume means more scams, and push notifications lag 3–10 minutes behind publication.

2Fotocasa — best for private landlords

Spain's second-largest portal and especially strong in Catalonia. Around 40% of Fotocasa listings never appear on Idealista, mostly from private landlords. Best for: supplementing Idealista to catch exclusives. Watch out for: ~60% overlap with Idealista on agency listings.

3Badi — best for rooms and shared flats

Purpose-built for room rentals and coliving, with better tenant profiles than Idealista's room section. Rooms typically run €450–700/month. Best for: students and young professionals on a budget. Watch out for: rooms only, limited to major cities.

4Habitaclia — strong in Catalonia

Owned by Adevinta (same as Fotocasa) and historically strong in Catalonia and the Barcelona area. Best for: a quick local cross-check. Watch out for: roughly 93% inventory overlap with Fotocasa — if you already use Fotocasa, it adds little.

5Spotahome — book mid/long-term remotely

Lists verified mid- to long-term rentals with video tours, so you can book from abroad without an in-person viewing. Best for: expats arriving from another country. Watch out for: service fees and a smaller, more curated inventory than the big portals.

6HousingAnywhere — student and expat rentals

A booking platform popular with international students and remote workers, with verified listings and online contracts. Best for: securing a place before you land in Barcelona. Watch out for: tenant service fees, fewer local private-landlord deals.

7Facebook groups — fast, private, riskier

Groups like "Pisos en Barcelona" and expat housing groups surface private-landlord listings that never hit the portals. Best for: moving quickly on direct-from-landlord offers. Watch out for: the highest scam rate of any channel — never pay before viewing in person.

8Wallapop — occasional private listings

Primarily a second-hand marketplace, but private landlords occasionally post rentals here that aren't elsewhere. Best for: an opportunistic extra check. Watch out for: no rental-specific filters, variable quality.

9pisos.com & yaencontre — secondary portals

Smaller general portals that aggregate agency and some private listings. Best for: filling gaps after the big three. Watch out for: heavy overlap with Idealista/Fotocasa, smaller inventory.

10Milanuncios — classifieds (use with caution)

A classified-ads site with cheap and occasionally exclusive private listings. Best for: bargain hunters willing to vet carefully. Watch out for: the highest scam risk among mainstream options — treat every listing skeptically.
